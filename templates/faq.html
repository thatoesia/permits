FAQs

To apply for a work permit in Botswana, you need to submit an application to the Botswana Immigration and Labour Ministry. The application should include necessary documents such as a job offer letter, valid passport, medical certificate, and relevant fees. The ministry will review your application and determine the eligibility for a work permit.

To obtain a residence permit in Botswana, you must meet certain requirements, which include a valid reason for residency, such as employment, investment, or family ties. You will need to provide supporting documents, such as a valid passport, proof of employment or investment, and police clearance certificates. The Immigration and Labour Ministry will assess your application based on these requirements.

The processing time for a work permit application in Botswana can vary. It generally takes several weeks to a few months for the Immigration and Labour Ministry to review and process the application. The processing time may depend on various factors, including the completeness of your application, the workload of the ministry, and any additional requirements or verifications needed.

Yes, it is possible to extend your work permit in Botswana. You should apply for an extension before your current work permit expires. The application process typically involves submitting the necessary documents, such as a letter from your employer, updated employment contract, and any other required supporting documents. The Immigration and Labour Ministry will assess your application for the extension.
